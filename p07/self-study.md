## B. GRAMMAR SELF-STUDY

### B1. Grammar Summary: Comparatives and Superlatives

#### Comparatives

**Use:** Comparing TWO things

**Form:**
- One-syllable adjectives: adjective + **-er** + than
- Two+ syllable adjectives: **more** + adjective + than
- Irregular: good ‚Üí **better**, bad ‚Üí **worse**

**IT Examples:**
- "AWS is **larger than** Azure in market share."
- "SaaS is **easier to implement than** IaaS."
- "Cloud storage is **more cost-effective than** on-premises servers."
- "This solution provides **better** security than the previous one."

**Comparative Phrases:**
- twice as fast as...
- significantly more expensive than...
- far more reliable than...
- slightly better than...
- much more scalable than...

---

#### Superlatives

**Use:** Comparing THREE or more things, or identifying THE BEST/WORST

**Form:**
- One-syllable adjectives: **the** + adjective + **-est**
- Two+ syllable adjectives: **the most** + adjective
- Irregular: good ‚Üí **the best**, bad ‚Üí **the worst**

**IT Examples:**
- "AWS is **the largest** cloud provider in the world."
- "GCP offers **the most advanced** AI tools."
- "This is **the best** solution for our company."
- "Azure has **the strongest** Microsoft integration."

---

#### Articles in Technical Context

| Article | Use | Examples |
|---------|-----|----------|
| **a/an** | First mention, general | "We need **a** virtual machine." / "We signed **an** SLA." |
| **the** | Specific, known | "**The** SLA we signed guarantees 99.9% uptime." |
| **√ò** (no article) | General concepts, uncountable | "**Cloud computing** is transforming business." |

**Important:** Use "**an**" before SLA because we pronounce it "es-el-ay" (starts with vowel sound).
